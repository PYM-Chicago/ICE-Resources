Chicago: Know Your Rights Against ICE

About

This page was developed by the Chicago chapter of the Palestinian Youth Movement and is a collection of links to resources on knowing your rights and preparedness against Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Customs and Border Patrol (CBP) for those living in the Chicago area and beyond. While these links are helpful for anyone, we tried our best to aggregate links here that would especially help the Arab and Muslim community.

We recognize that knowing your rights isn’t enough protection against the lawless thugs that have been terrorizing our city and abducting our loved ones and neighbors. However, knowing your rights is critical to ensure you do not fall into the traps they set for us and allow for legal recourse after the fact. It is also essential to make sure you and your family are prepared for the possibility of an abduction and have a plan in place for what to do should it happen to you.

We commend those among our community who have bravely stood up to protect each other from ICE terror and to fight back against them however they can. Please join your local rapid response groups, stay aware of your surroundings, and join the fight to protect our community. Talk to your neighbors, families, and friends, and get organized.

Community ICE Respose

  • If you believe you are witnessing ICE activity, please call ICIRR’s 24-Hour Family Support Hotline at 1-855-HELP-MY-FAMILY (1-855-435-7693) Live operators are available:

    6 AM - 9 PM every day in English and Spanish

    9 AM - 5 PM Monday-Friday in Arabic, Chinese, Dari, Korean, Pashto, Polish, Russian and Ukrainian.

    If you call outside of those times, you can expect a call back within 48 hours.

  • Join the IL Eyes on ICE state-wide text network to receive alerts about ICE activity near you, important updates, and ways to organize and get involved

  • Download the Red Dot ICE tracking app to see where ICE activity has been reported nationwide

  • Immigrant Defense Project resources about ICE raid tactics and check out their ICE raids toolkit

  • Sign up to join Pilsen Unidos por Nuestro Orgullo’s (PUÑO) Migra Watch team to learn how to identify federal agents, document ICE activity, and support immigrants in our communities

Organizations

Illinois Coalition for Immigrant & Refugee Rights (ICIRR)

  • ICIRR is dedicated to promoting the rights of immigrants and refugees to full and equal participation in the civic, cultural, social, and political life of our diverse society.
  • Training and deployment of Rapid Response Teams to situations where ICE is spotted trying to detain people
  • Family Support Hotline (FSN): 24 hotline for people to call when their family or friends are being detained (1-855-435-7693)
  • Know your rights trainings and resources
  • Arabic know-your-rights wallet size cards

Arab American Action Network (AAAN)

  • AAAN is a grassroots nonprofit, our strategies include community organizing, advocacy, education, providing social services, leadership development, cultural outreach and forging productive relationships with other communities.
  • AAAN offers social services in Arabic and advocate for immigrants
  • Volunteer with their ICIRR RR team

Immigrant Defense Project

  • IDP was founded over 20 years ago to combat an emerging human rights crisis: the targeting of immigrants for mass imprisonment and deportation.
  • IDP offers legal advice and trainings
  • Know-your-rights resources including materials in Arabic
  • ICE raids tactics guide and toolkit
